About Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is digital currency. It can be exchanged online for various goods and services. This virtual currency is secured by cryptography, which is where cryptocurrency gets its name. They work through blockchain, a highly secure database that keeps record of transactions in the form of a chain.

There are over 10,000 publicly traded cryptocurrencies, and as of August 2021, the total value of those cryptocurrencies are more than $1.8 trillion!

"Should I start using and trading crypto?" you may be asking. Well, let's look at some of the positives and negatives.

Pros

  • Cryptocurrency trades typically operate on a peer-to-peer networking structure. Traditional business deals usually involve paperwork, agents, representatives, commissions, and just overall confusion. Crypto transactions eliminate that by "cutting out the middle men".

  • Unlike traditional bank/credit transactions, arrangements using cryptocurrency often involve little to no transaction fees. This is beneficial for heavy shoppers, as fees can definitely stack up.

  • International trade is made less complicated through the use of cryptocurrencies. Not only is crypto not currently recognized as legal tender, but the peer-to-peer structure of the trade makes overseas transfers and transactions much less overwhelming for the users.

    • Cons

  • The mining done for cryptocurrency consumes a large amount of energy, which heavily impacts the environment. Bitcoin mining alone produces over 39 megatons of CO2 annually, and was estimated to raise global temperatures by 2°C (around 35°F) in the next 20 years.

  • Because cryptocurrency is not considered legal tender, the number of businesses (both online and real-life) at which they're accepted is still very limited, despite its growing acceptance within the past few years.

  • Cryptocurrencies are assets of private sectors (some more private than others) with no official regulation or governmental oversight on these assets. Some may use this to their advantage by exploiting or scamming unsuspecting users.


    • Use this information (and the plethora of information throughout this website) to make the decision. Enjoy!
